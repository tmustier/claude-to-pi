# Unslop profile for startup SaaS landing pages.

Generated from 20 HTML samples and screenshots.

---

## Layout patterns to avoid

- Do not default to the fixed top navbar with logo left, 3-4 links, and one CTA button on the right.
- Do not use blurred glass nav chrome as the default header treatment.
- Do not default to the standard SaaS stack: hero -> proof strip or stats -> features -> pricing -> testimonials or comparison -> closing CTA.
- Do not make every hero a centered headline block floating in a max-width column with two buttons underneath.
- Do not center a tiny amount of pricing content inside a tall, mostly empty page.
- Do not add a stats row under the hero as a reflex.
- Do not add a logo bar or trust strip just because the page feels empty.
- Do not make the page feel "premium" by stretching weak content into giant viewport-height sections.
- Do not leave huge dead-air bands between sections. Sparse content on tall sections reads as unfinished, not elegant.

## Visual defaults to avoid

- Do not rely on radial glows, blurred orbs, or haze behind the hero as your main visual idea.
- Do not stack `linear-gradient` and `radial-gradient` everywhere just to make the page feel designed.
- Do not default technical products to charcoal/ink backgrounds with cyan, teal, lime, or purple glow.
- Do not default friendly products to white/cream backgrounds with sky, amber, sage, or coral accents.
- Do not use gradient text on the key word in the H1 as the main flourish.
- Do not use frosted glass cards or translucent panels as default polish.
- Do not add subtle grid textures, dot fields, or fake noise just to fill space.
- Do not default stripped-down pricing pages to a pale beige or off-white canvas with a single white content card floating in the middle.

## Typography defaults to avoid

- Do not reach for Inter by default.
- Do not fake originality by pairing one tasteful serif display face with an otherwise generic sans system.
- Do not switch to JetBrains Mono, DM Mono, Space Mono, or similar just because the product is for developers.
- Do not use typography as a brand shortcut while leaving the layout completely generic.

## Component patterns to avoid

- Do not default pricing to three vertical cards with the middle tier featured.
- Do not present the same three tiers twice by showing cards first and then repeating them in a comparison table.
- Do not use a `Most Popular` badge as the automatic way to make pricing feel real.
- Do not default testimonials to five stars, one quote paragraph, and a round avatar or initials chip.
- Do not default FAQ to identical hairline rows with a plus icon on the far right.
- Do not isolate the money-back guarantee in a tiny rounded badge at the bottom as a stock trust patch.
- Do not patch trust with compliance badges unless they are actually needed.
- Do not throw in proof metrics like uptime, teams, revenue retained, or response times unless they are grounded in the prompt.
- Do not use the same sticky CTA/footer banner that asks the user one last time at the bottom of the page.

## Copy patterns to avoid

- Do not use `Trusted by ...`, `Loved by ...`, or near-equivalent trust lines by default.
- Do not reuse floating proof numbers like `2,400+`, `99.9%`, `4.9/5`, or similar generic credibility bait.
- Do not use `Built for ...` as your stock framing line.
- Do not use `Everything your team needs`, `Everything your front desk needs`, or any close variant.
- Do not use `Ready to ...?` as the closing CTA headline.
- Do not use `Simple, honest pricing`, `Plans that scale`, or other stock pricing headings.
- Do not default CTA copy to `Get Started`, `Start Free Trial`, `Start for Free`, `Book a Demo`, `Request Demo`, `Talk to Sales`, or `Watch Demo`.

## Code-level defaults to avoid

- Do not lean on `position: fixed` plus `backdrop-filter` as the default nav recipe.
- Do not build the hero with absolutely positioned glow blobs and call it done.
- Do not use `background-clip: text` as the standard headline effect.
- Do not hide thin section ideas behind gradients, blur, shadows, and fade-up animation.
- Do not use viewport-height sizing to manufacture drama when the section content is minimal.

## Domain-specific traps

- If the page is for devtools, do not automatically switch to dark charcoal, cyan accents, code panes, and monospace details.
- If the page is for healthcare, HR, or SMB operations software, do not automatically switch to cream backgrounds, soft blue gradients, and "friendly trustworthy" serif headlines.
- If the page is for enterprise/security, do not automatically paste in SOC 2, GDPR, ISO, and "Talk to Sales" just because the prompt sounds serious.

---

If you catch yourself reaching for any of these defaults, stop. Do not swap one stock SaaS move for another stock SaaS move. Break the pattern and make a genuinely different page.
